AI Prompt Engineering

This section presents AI prompt engineering as a practical portfolio capability: designing clear instructions, improving output structure, reducing ambiguity, and building reusable prompts for workplace productivity.

Capability

Prompt Design Principles

  • Set a clear role and objective for the AI.
  • Define context, audience, tone, and output format.
  • Specify constraints, exclusions, and decision logic.
  • Request structured output for easier review and reuse.
  • Iterate prompts based on quality gaps and results.
Use Cases

Where Prompt Engineering Helps

  • Summarizing audit findings and trend insights
  • Drafting professional emails, MOM notes, and observations
  • Creating calibration notes or reviewer coaching feedback
  • Generating structured blog posts and portfolio content
  • Converting complex policy logic into simpler guidance
Sample Prompt

Professional Reframing Prompt

Use this kind of prompt to transform raw issue notes into concise professional observations:

Act as a professional quality analyst. Reframe the following audit note into one concise and formal paragraph. Keep the tone objective, accurate, and action-oriented. Explain what was missed, why the decision was incorrect, and what the correct action should have been. If the case is ambiguous, mention that support should have been sought.

Input: [paste raw audit note here]
Output style: single paragraph, professional, no bullets.
Sample Prompt

LinkedIn / Blog Content Prompt

Use this type of prompt to create thought leadership posts from your work experience:

Act as a professional content writer for LinkedIn. Create a strong post for a Quality Auditing Analyst. Topic: [insert topic]. Tone: professional and insightful. Include a hook, 3 practical takeaways, and a short closing line. Keep it easy to read, high-value, and suitable for a professional audience. Do not exaggerate claims.
Sample Prompt

Prompt Template for Structured Summaries

A reusable format for summaries, trackers, observations, or meeting notes:

Role: Business analyst / quality analyst
Task: Summarize the provided content.
Context: [describe file, tracker, meeting, or discussion]
Audience: Leadership / team lead / operations / stakeholders
Format:
1. Executive summary
2. Key observations
3. Risks or quality gaps
4. Recommended actions
Tone: concise, formal, data-driven
Length: 150–250 words
